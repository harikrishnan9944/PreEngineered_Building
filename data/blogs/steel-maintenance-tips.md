# 5 Essential Maintenance Tips for Industrial Steel Structures

Steel is highly resilient, but industrial environments expose structural columns and beams to chemical fumes, moisture, and extreme load dynamics. Maintaining your steel assets increases building lifespans and guarantees workplace safety.

## 1. Implement High-Performance Coatings

Corrosion is the greatest threat to structural steel. In areas with high humidity or chemical fumes, a standard single-coat paint is insufficient.
- **Shotblast Preparation**: Ensure steel is cleaned to SA 2.5 standard before applying paint.
- **Epoxy Primers**: Apply zinc-rich epoxy primers to create an electrochemical barrier.
- **Polyurethane Topcoats**: Use PU coatings to prevent UV damage and weather wear.

## 2. Annual Bolt Tension Audits

Industrial factories with overhead crane operations undergo constant dynamic vibrations. Over time, connection bolts can lose their specified tension.
- Schedule annual checks on critical structural nodes.
- Use calibrated torque wrenches to verify high-strength bolt status.

## 3. Verify Joint and Sealant Integrity

Water ingress at joints is a major cause of hidden rust.
- Inspect roof sheet overlaps and wall cladding joints.
- Replace worn neoprene wash washers on fastners.
- Reapply high-grade silicone sealants at junctions.

## Maintenance Schedule Checklist

| Component | Inspection Frequency | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Column Bases | Every 6 Months | Check for water logging and mechanical impacts |
| Crane Girders | Every 6 Months | Perform weld inspection and alignment audits |
| Roof Cladding | Annually (Pre-monsoon) | Clear debris, leaves, and verify gutter drainage |
| Anchor Bolts | Every 12 Months | Check for concrete spalling and rust signs |

## 4. Conduct NDT (Non-Destructive Testing)

For older plants, look beneath the surface.
- Use **Ultrasonic Thickness Gauging** to verify if corrosion has reduced load capacity.
- Run **Magnetic Particle Testing** on heavy stress joints to detect micro-cracks before they fail.

## 5. Prevent Dissimilar Metal Contact

Galvanic corrosion occurs when two different metals (e.g., steel and copper) touch in the presence of moisture.
- Ensure rubber isolation spacers are installed between different metal connections.
